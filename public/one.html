INCLUSION CRITERIA

Temporal Requirements

  • Trial initiated after March 12, 2019 (FDA biomarker guidance effective date)
  • FDA approval or regulatory decision through June 2025
  • Complete enrollment data available in public filings

Biomarker Criteria

  • Prospective biomarker-driven patient selection (per FDA guidance)
  • Genetic, protein, or molecular biomarker specification
  • Clear biomarker-positive/negative population definition
  • Documented enrollment by biomarker status

Data Quality

  • FDA Summary Basis of Approval available
  • Approval package accessible via FDA.gov
  • Published clinical trial results in peer-reviewed literature
  • ClinicalTrials.gov registration with results

EXCLUSION CRITERIA

Post-hoc Analyses

  • Retrospective biomarker identification after enrollment
  • Subgroup analyses not part of original protocol
  • Biomarker evaluation added during trial conduct
  • Post-marketing pharmacogenomic discoveries

Non-qualifying Designs

  • Diagnostic-only biomarkers without treatment prediction
  • Safety-only exclusion criteria without efficacy prediction
  • Convenience samples without systematic selection

Insufficient Documentation

  • Missing biomarker enrollment breakdown
  • Proprietary data without public disclosure
  • Incomplete FDA approval documentation
  • Failed trials without regulatory submission

Research Challenges in Psychiatry

Barriers to New Trials

Diagnostic Heterogeneity: DSM-5 symptom-based diagnoses create diverse populations, reducing effect sizes.

High Placebo Response: Elevated placebo effects dilute treatment signals, increasing costs.

Lack of Validated Biomarkers: No validated biomarkers lead to 100% biomarker-negative trials.

Low Success Rates: Historically low success rates deter investment in new drugs.

Clinical Impact

Pharmacogenomic Guidance: Pre-2019 trials provide FDA-approved dosing for CYP2D6/CYP2C19.

Moderate Enrichment: 6-15% biomarker-positive populations vs oncology’s 100% positive trials.

Historical Precedent: Pre-2019 trials inform current practice and future designs.

Discovery Focus: Post-2019 trials prioritize biomarker discovery for future stratification.

Trial Burden Impact Analysis: Understanding the 5-10x Cost Difference

AI INSIGHT: Trial Burden Mechanics

Trial burden increases exponentially when biomarker-negative patients are included because: (1) Effect dilution requires larger sample sizes for statistical power, (2) Longer enrollment periods due to screening ratios, (3) Higher operational costs from managing larger trials, (4) Increased failure risk from mixed signals.

What is "Trial Burden Impact"?

Definition: Trial burden impact measures how biomarker enrichment strategies affect the size, cost, timeline, and success probability of clinical trials.

Key Components:

  • Sample Size Burden: How many patients needed for statistical power
  • Timeline Burden: How long to enroll and complete the study
  • Cost Burden: Total financial investment required
  • Risk Burden: Probability of trial failure due to diluted signals

Why it matters: Including large numbers of biomarker-negative patients (predicted non-responders) dramatically increases all burden components without improving drug safety or efficacy assessment.

Statistical Formula Documentation

Sample Size Calculation:

n = 2 × [(Zα/2 + Zβ)² × σ²] / δ²

Zα/2 = 1.96 (95% CI), Zβ = 1.28 (90% power)

Diluted Effect in All-Comer:

δobserved = p × δpositive + (1-p) × δnegative

p = biomarker prevalence

Statistical methodology per FDA Guidance E9 and Simon & Maitournam (2004)
