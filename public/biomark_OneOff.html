Trial Burden Impact Analysis: Understanding the 5-10x Cost Difference

AI INSIGHT: Trial Burden Mechanics

Trial burden increases exponentially when biomarker-negative patients are included because: (1) Effect dilution requires larger sample sizes for statistical power, (2) Longer enrollment periods due to screening ratios, (3) Higher operational costs from managing larger trials, (4) Increased failure risk from mixed signals. This compounds to create 5-10x cost differences between enriched and mixed population designs.

What is "Trial Burden Impact"?

Definition: Trial burden impact measures how biomarker enrichment strategies affect the size, cost, timeline, and success probability of clinical trials.

Key Components:

  • Sample Size Burden: How many patients needed for statistical power
  • Timeline Burden: How long to enroll and complete the study
  • Cost Burden: Total financial investment required
  • Risk Burden: Probability of trial failure due to diluted signals

Why it matters: Including large numbers of biomarker-negative patients (predicted non-responders) dramatically increases all burden components without improving drug safety or efficacy assessment.

Statistical Foundation: Why 5-10x Trial Burden Occurs

Power Analysis Mathematics

Sample Size Formula: n = 2(Zα + Zβ)2 / δ2

Where:

  • Zα = 1.96 (Type I error = 0.05)
  • Zβ = 0.84 (Power = 80%)
  • δ = Effect size (key variable)

Mixed Population Effect: δmixed = (p × δpos) + ((1-p) × δneg)

Result: Smaller δmixed → Exponentially larger sample size

Real-World Example
