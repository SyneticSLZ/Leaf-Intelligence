Antibody-Oligonucleotide Conjugate (AOC™) Platform

i
Revolutionary Platform:
The AOC platform represents a breakthrough in targeted RNA delivery, combining:
• Specificity: Antibody targets specific cell types
• Potency: Oligonucleotide modulates RNA processing
• Durability: Antibody half-life extends dosing intervals
• Safety: Targeted delivery reduces off-target effects

First-in-Class: AOC-1044 is the first AOC to demonstrate clinical efficacy in humans

1. TfR1 Targeting

i
Transferrin Receptor 1 (TfR1):
• Highly expressed on muscle cells (100,000+ copies/cell)
• Essential for iron uptake in rapidly dividing/metabolically active cells
• Provides muscle-specific targeting mechanism
• Also expressed on cardiac muscle

Binding: AOC-1044 antibody binds TfR1 without interfering with natural transferrin function

Anti-TfR1 mAb binds transferrin receptor 1 on muscle cells with high specificity

2. Endocytosis

i
Cellular Uptake:
• TfR1 binding triggers clathrin-mediated endocytosis
• AOC-1044 transported into endosomes
• Natural cellular machinery used for delivery
• Process takes 15-30 minutes

Advantage: Hijacks natural iron uptake pathway for efficient drug delivery

Receptor-mediated internalization delivers AOC-1044 into muscle cytoplasm

3. PMO Release

i
Controlled Release:
• Proprietary linker cleaved by cellular enzymes
• PMO payload released into cytoplasm
• Free PMO translocates to cell nucleus
• Process optimized for sustained release

Innovation: Linker technology enables predictable, sustained PMO delivery over days to weeks

Linker cleavage releases PMO payload for nuclear translocation and pre-mRNA binding

4. Exon Skipping

i
RNA Modulation:
• PMO binds to exon 44 splice enhancer sequences
• Blocks splice site recognition during pre-mRNA processing
• Results in exon 44 exclusion from mature mRNA
• Restores dystrophin reading frame

Outcome: Produces shortened but functional dystrophin protein, converting severe DMD to milder BMD-like phenotype

PMO binds exon 44 splice sites, causing exclusion during mRNA processing

Comprehensive Preclinical Research

Non-Human Primate Studies

i
Why NHP Studies Matter:
Non-human primates (cynomolgus monkeys) are the gold standard for DMD research because:
• Similar muscle physiology to humans
• TfR1 expression patterns comparable to humans
• Required by FDA for biologics development
• Predictive of human safety and efficacy

GLP Compliance: Good Laboratory Practice standards ensure data quality for regulatory submissions

Study Design & Validation

  • • 49 cynomolgus monkeys across dose groups i
    Study Scale:
    49 animals across multiple dose groups (5, 15, 45 mg/kg PMO44) represents one of the largest NHP exon skipping studies ever conducted. Group sizes of 8-12 animals per dose provide robust statistical power for safety and efficacy assessments.
  • • 9-month treatment duration with excellent tolerability i
    Chronic Safety Assessment:
    9 months of treatment (equivalent to several years in humans) with IV infusions every 4 weeks demonstrated no treatment-related adverse findings. This extensive safety database supports long-term human use.
  • • GLP-compliant studies supporting IND filing i
    Regulatory Standard:
    Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) compliance means studies follow strict quality standards required by FDA for Investigational New Drug (IND) applications. This ensures data integrity and regulatory acceptance.
  • • Validated translational biomarkers established i
    Biomarker Translation:
    CK, ALT, AST, and LDH biomarkers validated in NHPs successfully predicted the >80% CK reduction seen in human trials. This validates the translational relevance of the preclinical models.

EXPLORE44™ Phase 1/2 Trial (NCT05670730)

i
EXPLORE44 Trial Design:
This pivotal study represents the first human testing of the AOC platform:
• Two-part adaptive design allows dose optimization based on real-time data
• Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled ensures rigorous scientific standards
• IND clearance in September 2022 enabled rapid progression to clinic
• Primary completion in March 2025 with positive topline results

Significance: First study to demonstrate clinically meaningful exon skipping with antibody-oligonucleotide conjugates

Study Design

  • • Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled i
    Gold Standard Design: Double-blind, placebo-controlled trials eliminate bias and provide the highest quality evidence for regulatory approval. Randomization ensures balanced patient characteristics across treatment groups.
  • • Two-part adaptive design i
    Adaptive Design Benefits: Part A (healthy volunteers) informed optimal dosing for Part B (patients). This approach reduces risk and accelerates development by using early data to optimize later phases.
  • • IND cleared September 27, 2022 i
    IND Clearance: FDA's Investigational New Drug application clearance allows human testing. The September 2022 clearance enabled rapid progression from preclinical to clinical studies.
  • • Primary completion: March 2025
ClinicalTrials.gov

Part A: Healthy Volunteers

  • • 40 participants (ages 18-55) i
    Healthy Volunteer Phase: Testing in healthy volunteers first establishes safety and pharmacokinetics before exposing patients to risk. 40 participants across dose levels provides robust safety database.
  • • Single ascending doses: 1.5-20 mg/kg i
    Dose Escalation: Single ascending dose design starts with low, safe doses and gradually increases. The 1.5-20 mg/kg range identified 5-10 mg/kg as optimal for patient studies.
  • • Safety, PK, muscle PMO levels
  • • Completed December 2023

Part B: DMD44 Patients

  • • 24 patients (ages 7-27) i
    Patient Population: 24 DMD44 patients represents a significant portion of this rare population. Age range 7-27 includes both ambulatory and non-ambulatory patients, providing broad applicability data.
  • • Multiple doses: 5, 10, 20 mg/kg Q6W i
    Dosing Strategy: Q6W (every 6 weeks) dosing based on AOC platform's extended half-life. Multiple dose levels (5, 10, 20 mg/kg) allow dose-response analysis and optimal dose selection.
  • • Ambulatory and non-ambulatory
  • • 3-month treatment period

Understanding DMD and Exon 44 Skipping

What is DMD?

Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy is a genetic disorder causing progressive muscle degeneration. It affects 1 in 3,500-5,000 male births and is caused by mutations in the dystrophin gene.

How Exon Skipping Works

AOC-1044 uses antisense oligonucleotides to "skip" the faulty exon 44 during protein production, allowing cells to make shortened but functional dystrophin protein.

Why This Matters

Currently, patients with exon 44-amenable mutations have no specific treatments. AOC-1044 could be the first targeted therapy for this subset of DMD patients.
